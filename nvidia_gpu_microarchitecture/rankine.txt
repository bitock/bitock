
Rankine is the codename for a GPU microarchitecture developed by Nvidia, and released in 2003, as the successor to the Kelvin microarchitecture.

It was named with reference to Macquorn Rankine and used with the GeForce FX series.








Architecture			Rankine
Fabrication process		150 nm, 140 nm, 130 nm
